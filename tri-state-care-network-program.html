Tri-State Care Network Program

Caring Partners International has developed and supported the Tri-State Care Network - a coalition comprising local churches, drop-in centers, halfway houses, and various faith-based community organizations. This network is dedicated to providing essential healthcare supplies and personal hygiene products to the most vulnerable populations in our local communities. Caring Partners International itself does not directly engage with patients or clients; instead, our role is to empower these local entities to serve their communities effectively. We depend on these organizations to distribute the necessary supplies directly to their neighbors in need.
In addition, CPI and its partner organizations actively involve community volunteers in the processes of receiving, organizing, and distributing these supplies. This collaborative effort not only addresses immediate needs but also strengthens community bonds through volunteerism in Southwest Ohio.

Community Needs

Many individuals and families within the Ohio/Kentucky/Indiana Tri-State area struggle to secure basic personal care and hygiene items due to strained household budgets. Consequently, essential items often neglected include:

  • Home Health Products (e.g., Wheelchairs, Walkers, Crutches)
  • Dental Hygiene Products (Toothbrushes, toothpaste, floss, mouthwash)
  • Bath & Hand Soap
  • Lotions (Body & Hand)
  • Toilet Paper & Tissues
  • Shampoo & Conditioners
  • Feminine Hygiene Products
  • Baby Care Products (Diapers, Wipes)
  • Linens (Sheets, Pillowcases, Towels, Washcloths)
  • Nutritional Supplements (Vitamins, etc.)

Economic pressures have a significant irnpact on personal care and nutritional health, particularly among children.

Leveraging over two decades of experience in sourcing, warehousing, and distributing medical, personal care, and nutritional supplies, CPI efficiently addresses these challenges and supports the well­ being of our local communities.

IMPORTANT INFORMATION

No controlled substances, expired products, previously prescribed medicines, or opened medications are accepted. OTC Medicines should have a shelf life of at least six months due to shipping schedules and storage requirements. It is against the law for Caring Partners to accept medications prescribed to individuals.
